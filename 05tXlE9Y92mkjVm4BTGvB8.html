High sugar after eating: what to do?

The reason for this post was your comments. The topic I consider interesting and useful for many! Guys, join the discussion and share
own example: who has how the sugars behave an hour after eating and
what algorithm of actions would you use in the situation described below you.
⠀
☝ Situation:
Before lunch sugar 7,2.
Lunch: Borscht, two loaves of bread, a Cup of black tea with natural stevia.
Teasing on food 4 units.
SK in an hour - 13.4. Bit high on sugar 1 unit.
Sugar in 15 minutes - 10.0.
After 30 minutes, SK - 3.2.
⠀
Debriefing the situation:
⠀
1️ ⃣ I See no pause before eating (Source 1). as a consequence-high blood sugar an hour after eating.
⠀
After we eat, the process of assimilation of food begins. Carbohydrates are one of the first foods to be digested. They are absorbed into the blood mainly in the form of glucose. That is why after eating, all people experience hyperglycemia - an increase in blood glucose levels higher than the normal value.
⠀
In this situation, the peak of insulin processing did not coincide with the postprandial peak. And this could have been avoided by taking a pause.


2️⃣ Injecting short-acting insulin to reduce blood pressure an hour after a meal is a mistake. In the body was active insulin (about 2.5-2.2 units), introduced at lunch, but to it added another 1 unit of insulin. Taking into account the carbohydrate coefficient of a person of 1 u of insulin to 1 XE, the subsequent hypoglycemia in this case is a natural result of pricking.
⠀

Yes, we all have different organisms and different situations. But before you make a shot of additional insulin to reduce SC just an hour after a meal, be sure to take into account:
⠀
✔ App is active insulin. The time of processing short-acting insulin from 2 to 5 hours.
✔ I GI of foods. If the GI is high, it will give a jump in blood sugar, if you do not correctly "catch" it with insulin due to a pause between teasing and eating.
⠀
