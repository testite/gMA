Spine at the Desk

Every fourth person is afraid of spinal diseases. But this fear does not affect proper posture and care of the body. From a young age, most people load their backs with weights in the form of school backpacks, and then - spend several days at a time hours in a sitting position and avoids physical activity.

Consequently, most people not only have posture defects, but also experience associated pain and malaise. What to do to keep your spine in the best shape after working at your Desk for a few hours the form?

Social and civilizational changes affect the growth of employment in the service sector. Being in offices becomes a universal problem. People spend their days poring over papers, keyboards, and calculating machines.

All of them, over time, more and more begin to complain of problems with the spine. Research on workplace ergonomics clearly shows that the situation is very far from ideal.

That is why spine diseases, injuries and other diseases of the musculoskeletal system have long replaced the typical occupational diseases that are the result of physical work, which were previously observed in mining, metallurgy, or manufacturing.

Sitting is harmful and inconvenient.

Being in a sitting position is not a natural pose for a person. That is why activities directly related to this position of the body simply cannot be performed healthy for the spine.

People who constantly work in an office environment are constantly causing harm to their body. The wrong position of the body leads to tension and stress on the spine, neck and shoulder muscles, as well as - adversely affects the digestive system, blocks a full breath and causes reduction of oxygen reserves in the bloodstream. This leads to a General fatigue not only of the body, but also of the mind.

The position of the hands is also very important. Leaning your elbow on the countertop, you provoke pressure on the ulnar nerve and numbness in your fingers. In turn, relying on the wrists, you can provoke carpal tunnel syndrome channel's.

Therefore, in order not to harm yourself, you need to remember about several factors. The first of these is a straight back and legs. Because you sit at an angle of 20-30 degrees, your back muscles contract.

The support in the form of upper limbs removes a significant part of the load from the back. This is a " straight back with maintaining its natural curvature."

It should be remembered that this position excludes the use of a chair back. Equally important is the correct position of the head, which should not tilt in any direction. Install the monitor at eye level and do not forget to take care of spine hygiene. Short and easy steps are required. regular absence from work or gymnastics.

If you are sitting, then…

1. the Correct posture should be dynamic. It is necessary to change the position of the body as often as possible, so that the muscles will move. This will help to avoid their constant stress.

2. Get an orthopedic chair. Also you can just sit on a large rubber ball — such seats provoke the mobilization of all the muscles, and force you to constantly change your body position.

3. You must take care of the stable support of your hands on the table. However, you should remember not to use your wrists and elbows. You only need to lean on the countertop forearms.

4. the Screen should be placed directly in front of the eyes, at a distance of about 40 cm from the face. You should look slightly down – in the center of the monitor, so as not to turn your neck. Otherwise, it can lead to muscle contracture that causes pain.

5. Despite the fact that you are often sitting with your legs bent under the it is worth trying to make sure that this angle is the largest. This can be achieved by using a footrest.

6. Several times per hour, you need to get up from the table to stretch and perform short exercises.

7. it is Worth considering the location of the printer or scanner, which will make you stand up and go for documents.

Short office exercises and more…

1. Tighten your legs and move them without getting up. This is the easiest way to relax tense muscles.

2. stand up Every 30 minutes and turn your head in all directions. Gently but firmly turn it to the right, left, up and down. Take a deep breath and relax.

3. Every hour, perform an exercise consisting of pressing the chin on the neck – do the so-called "double" chin.

4. once a day, place your hand on your forehead, nape, and temples (one at a time) and press your head on them for 5 seconds each time. Perform a few short series of exercises.

5. Stand for a while on one leg – you can hold on to any surface. And tighten your buttocks. Contrary to popular belief, taking care of a good spine condition also requires strengthening the abdominal and buttock muscles.

6. If possible, do the exercise, it consists of crossing the arms and bending over while simultaneously straightening the limbs. In this position, you should hold for a few seconds and perform it several times.

7. walk at least a kilometer a day.

We warn you about mandatory consultation with a medical professional a specialist!
